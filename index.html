Operating Model

Infrastructure is valuable when it improves the operation.

This page is built around a point of view: cloud systems should be judged by how well they reduce operational drag, make change safer, and give teams better control.

My owner’s view of engineering comes from building systems I needed to run real businesses. The architecture matters, but only after the workflow is understood.

01

Start with the workflow.

What is being requested, approved, released, escalated, measured, or handed off? That tells me what the system has to protect.

02

Design for the operating path.

I prioritize reviewability, rollback, least-privilege access, auditability, cost controls, observability, and known failure modes.

03

Leave evidence behind.

Terraform, CI checks, runbooks, ADRs, teardown notes, security reasoning, and documented limitations make the work inspectable.
